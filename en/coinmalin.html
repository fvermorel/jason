The location of the tale

Jason and the Argonauts travelled extensively. Let’s review together where they went.

Mediterranean

Greeks are known as a seafaring people. In antiquity, they did not only inhabit the Greece that we know today: they also had established cities and colonies on numerous islands and on the shores of the Mediterranean.

D.R.

Iolcos

Iolcos is not a well-known town. In fact without Jason and the Argonauts, few people would know of it. It was told that it was founded by Cretheus, the son of the god of the wind: Aeolus. Today, she has a bit more than 2000 residents.

D.R.

The Aegean Sea

The Aegean Sea bears its name from the king Aegeus whose story you will learn when you read the book “Minos and Theseus”. She delimits Europe from the Middle East and we find many islands within it.

D.R.

The Sea of Marmara

The Sea of Marmara is small but very important because she connects the Mediterranean Sea with the black sea. It is closed by the Detroit of the Dardanelles and the straits of the Bosphorus. When they cross it, Jason and the Argonauts change worlds.

D.R.

The Black Sea

The sea is an inland Sea. She borders many European countries and the Middle East: Romania, Ukraine, Russia, Turkey etc.

D.R.

Colchis

On the east coast of the Black Sea is where one can find the kingdom of Colchis. Nowadays this region is part of Georgia.

D.R.

The boats throughout the ages

To fish, to travel or to conduct commerce, men have always needed boats that allow them to move on water. But over time, boats have made much progress.

Raft

The first crafts were simply tree trunks tied to one another. These are rafts. We find them in prehistory. Subsequently, men learned to hollow them out to make pirogues.

©Wikipedia

Pirogue

A Pirogue is a vessel built with a single tree trunk. It allows people to navigate rivers or near coasts but these are too small to challenge the open sea.

D.R.

Sailboat

When men began to build hulls, it became possible to put in place a mast with a sail and to use the wind to travel.

D.R.

Galley

When there is no wind or when one wants to go faster, then one must row. It is what Jason and the Argonauts do. But often it is the condemned or slaves who must row.

D.R.

Caravelle and Galion

In the middle ages, boats improved. It would soon be possible to travel long, going as far as... America with Christopher Columbus

D.R

Steam Boat

In the 19th century, steam power appears. Boats are equipped with a furnace that allows them to go faster and no longer be dependent on wind.

D.R.

Motor Boat

In the 19th century, boats improve. The “outboard” allows boats to go very fast. But they pollute and make a lot of noise.

D.R

Submarine

A submarine is a boat that … goes under water. Today they are mostly used for military or scientific purposes.
