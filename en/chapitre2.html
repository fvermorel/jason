Chapter 2
The expedition
Lemnos
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The Argonauts reached the island of Lemnos who was only inhabited by women. They were damned by Aphrodite: they smelled horribly and their smell had made their husbands run away. They were very distraught because they could not have children.
Castor and Pollux did a sacrifice to their father, Zeus, the king of gods and asked him to lift the curse. Out of love for his sons, Zeus accepted. The smell of the women of Lemnos dissipated. Their husbands returned and they all returned to a normal life. A few Argonauts were charmed by the Lemnian women and promised to return to marry them after their adventures were finished.
There are many islands in the Mediterranean of ancient Greeks and often they host strange creatures.
Sirens
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The Argonauts had been rowing for many days without seeing land. The dolphins and the birds had also disappeared and the wind was not blowing anymore. Suddenly, breaking the silence of the sea, a wonderful chant arose from deep within the sea. It confused the hearts of the heroes: some remembered the house of their childhood. Others remembered a young wife, abandoned for months with her children... They stopped to row to listen to the melodious song that awoke in them delicious memories.
Unfortunately, these were the terrible sirens, creatures half-fish half-woman and their voice was a dangerous trap. They were so pretty they were hard to resist: the sailors who heard them threw themselves overboard and ended up devoured by the monsters!
Orpheus understood the danger and brought out his harp. His song covered that of the sirens. The Argonauts regained their sense and once again began to row to leave the territory of the sirens.
The Giants
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The Argonauts stopped once again, this time on the island of the Bear. The king hosted them for a banquet and heard Jason tell his tale of the quest, of his adventures and the mysterious Golden Fleece. But during his absence, giants with 10 arms came out of the forest and attacked the boat! Fortunately, Hercules had stayed on board to keep watch over the boat. His lion skin rendered him invulnerable and he was even stronger than the monsters. He chased the giants away with blows of his cub and chased them with bows and arrows.
Hercules is used to fighting against monsters: Lern's hydra, the Nemean lion ...
The passage of the Bosphorus
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Our heroes approached the passage the most dangerous of their trip. To enter the black sea, they had to cross a narrow passage where rocks as large as mountains were moving constantly. If the Argo found itself stuck between two of these enormous rocks, it would be crushed and all the Argonauts would die.
The seer Tiresias gave this advice to Jason: “let a dove fly. Where she is worried to go, do not go yourself. When she goes straight ahead, have the Argonauts push on the oars with all their strength.”
Jason sent out the dove. Three times the white bird hesitated and went a different way. And three times the rocks knocked each other in a huge crash. And then the bird dove and flew straight ahead skimming the sea foam.
Orpheus sang the rhythm and Typhus pushed the wheel. The Argonauts rowed with all their might. The current was very strong and the waves beat maddeningly against the hull of the boat. But our heroes held the bearing and based on the dexterity of Typhus, they did not lose the dove from sight. They left the water of rock exactly as the two cliffs closed themselves behind them. The stern of the Argo was damaged and the dove lost a few feathers off its black tail.
But they were finally in the black sea.
