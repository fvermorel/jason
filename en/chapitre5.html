The end of the adventures
The return to Lolcos
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Helped by favorable winds that the Goddess Hera had raised, the Argonauts easily got back to Lolcos. They docked the Argo at the port. Their great boat had suffered much in their crossing. It was all worn, and many of the parts had been broken and fixed more than once. But it was still a formidable ship: with it, the Argonauts had accomplished the greatest expedition of all Greece.
Our heroes separated. Some went back to the island of Lemnos to find a wife. Others went back to their town where they became king. Others, finally, took back to the road for new adventures. Such a great regrouping of people, we would not see for a long time.
Jason with his wife Medea, went to the palace of Pelias to reclaim the throne.

The death of King Pelias
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
In Colchis or in Greece, the kings are the same: when it is time to own up to their word, they hesitate and find excuses. Pelias had believed that Jason and the Argonauts would die in their quests. The sumptuous Golden Fleece didn’t seem as beautiful after all. Anyway, it was less desirable than his throne.
Pelias multiplied his tricks to gain more time. He claimed that the omens were not favorable. Or that urgent matters demanded his attention. It was never the right time… In truth he wanted to wait so that all the Argonauts were sufficiently far from Lolcos. Then, he could easily get rid of the isolated Jason.
Once more, the magician skills of Medea allowed him to know the thoughts of the king. She was not one to let her husband be massacred. So she invited the daughters of Pelias at her place.
She did a demonstration of her talents. She put a very old mangy dog into a bath full of magical herbs. She washed him and rubbed him… and it was a young alert and lively animal that jumped out!
King Pelias was an old man, full of rheumatism. The princesses were delighted at the idea to give their father youth and vigor once again. They took the herbs that Medea had given them and had a bath prepared for him. Pelias went in …. And died instantly. Medea had sprung a trap on them.
Running again
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The kings’ daughters cried and yelled and called the guards still loyal to Pelias. A foreigner had killed the king. She must be punished, right away. Jason and Medea wanted to flee but the palace was surrounded by guards and a furious crowd. How to get away? The two lovers ran to hide in the attic.

The old woman, the goddess Hera, was waiting for them. She was not alone, at her sides was the Centaur Chiron, who had brought up Jason as a son. Jason and Medea climbed on his back and he left bounding above the crowd. The guards raced to the chase, but how could simple riders keep up with the speed of a centaur?
