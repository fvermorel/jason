The Harpies
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The Argonauts arrived in Thrace , the last stop before Colchis. The king of that country, Phineus was reduced to a state of starvation and extreme misery. When he wanted to eat, harpies, creatures half-women, half-bird would come down from the skies to sully his meal. Wherever he hid, they always found him.
Calais and Zetes, the sons of the god of wind , took flight and chased the harpies away. They scared the harpies so much that they fled forever and let Phineus be. He, to thank them, offered food and shelter to the Argonauts and gave them enough food to reach Colchis.
The fury of Zeus
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
But his rage did not abate. He had proof: humans were an evil species. They had to be eradicated from this world! Zeus commanded Aeolus, the God of Winds, to release torrents of rain on earth. Zeus commanded Poseidon, God of the Sea, to cover the earth with water. His waves covered houses, forests, and even the highest mountains.
Soon on earth, there was a sea storm and violent hurricanes. The great flood had begun. It would purge the earth of the dirt and meanness of humans.

The Argonauts finally landed in Colchis, the end of their voyage, but certainly not the end of their adventure! They indeed needed to regain the Golden Fleece.
When he landed on the dock, Jason recognized the old woman who helped him cross the river. The goddess Hera did not have a boat to be able to move about on the land of mortals! She was holding by the hand a small young boy with a mischievous look. She gave the baby in care to Jason and told him to take him to meet Aeëtes, the King of Colchis.
The Argonauts stayed on the docks and it is in the company of the small child that Jason went to the palace.

The Justs
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Deucalion and Pyrrha were a couple of fishermen. They lived a simple life. They were poor, but they never forgot to perform sacrifices for the gods. Right before Zeus unleashed his wrath, an oracle had told them to get into their boat. They had obeyed the command without objections. When the waters rose above the mountains, they were carried by their boat and did not drown.
Zeus saw them. They were the only survivors of the human race. He had only to lift his finger and his lightning bold would smite them. They were so fragile, so meek, that for once, the king of gods had pity upon them. He decided to spare them. Chained to his rock, Prometheus then knew he had foreseen correctly.

The princess Medea
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
At the entrance to the palace a young woman with golden hair and eyes dark as the night welcomed Jason. She was so beautiful that Jason’s mouth dropped, incapable even of remembering why he was there! It was Medea, the daughter of the King of Colchis.
The small child took out of his bag a small bow. With a dazzling speed, he pulled it back and fired a tiny arrow at Medea. Then he flew into the sky, carried by wings who had grown on his heels. It was Eros, god of love, but no one had seen what he did because he was hidden from the eyes of mortals except for Jason.
Medea, however, had now fallen deeply in love with Jason. While Jason had been separated from his faithful Argonauts, he was helped by a signfificant ally: the daughter of King of Colchis.

The rebirth of men
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
At the end of nine days and nine nights, Deucalion and Pyrrha reached a small island. It was the top of Mount Parnassus that crested above the flood waters. The water withdrew. The oracle told them that to repopulate mankind, they needed to throw the bones of their grandmother over their shoulders. They stood a moment in surprise, and then Pyrrha understood.: the grandmother was the earth, and its bones were stones.
When the water was completely withdrawn, Deucalion and Pyrrha came down the mountain. At each step, they grabbed a rock and threw it over their shoulders. Each time Deucalion would throw one, a man would arise from the earth; each time Pyrrha threw one, it was a woman that grew from the dirt: mankind was reborn!
