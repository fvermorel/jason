Chapter 1
The youth of Jason
Pelias and Aeson
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
All this happened a long long time ago in Greece in a town called Iolcus. At this time the gods lived amongst men and sometimes bore children with them. It is in this way that the god of wind, Aelous had for son Salmoneus, who in term had a daughter Tyro. Tyro married Cretheus, the king of the city of Iolcus and gave him two sons. The sons disliked one another and were named Pelias and Aeson, and it is with them that our story begins.
Pelias was stronger and meaner. At the death of their father, he seized the throne of Iolcus that should have gone to Aeson. Worrying about the punishment of the gods, he decided not to kill his brother. But a prophecy had warned him: “you will be dethroned by a descendant of Aelous that will present itself to you, one shoe on his foot.” And Aeson, had a son, a young baby named Jason.
A childhood in the mountains
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
To safeguard his child, Aeson pretended that his son had died and organized a fake funeral. But in secret he gave his son to his friend, the Centaur Chiron. Both of them left and went into the mountains where they lived for years, away from the sight of men and Pelias.
Chiron took Jason as his own son. He taught him music, poetry, astronomy and history. He taught him to fight with a sword, how to throw a disk and a javelin, how to shoot with a bow, how to run and grapple. He also taught, most importantly, to respect and honor the gods, regardless of their acts or intentions.
Prométhée est un titan, fils de Japet qui est lui même fils d’Ouranos, le ciel. Ca en fait le cousin de Zeus, comme vous pourrez l’apprendre dans notre livre “L’origine du monde”.
Towards Iolcus
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Now at 20 years of age, Jason was imposing. He was as handsome and tall as the biggest heroes of Greece touted in the Greek legends. The Centaur Chrion revealed that he was son of a king and that his father still lived, imprisoned at Iolcus by Pelias. His divine responsibility commanded him to leave to reclaim the throne. He left that same day.
On the road he ran into an old woman in front of a river she could not cross by herself. Jason hoisted her on his shoulder but weighted down; he lost his sandal during the crossing.
He arrived at Iolcus which was one of the largest and most prosperous cities in Greece. He presented himself to the King Pelias. Pelias recognized him even before he gave his name: the voyager with only one shot could only be the one from the prophecy.
Youpi ! Ca va mieux Je n'ai plus froid.
La toison d'or
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Pelias did not want to give up his crown, but he also could not oppose himself to the will of the gods. He pondered what to do next. As he had travelled much in his youth and knew of many legends, he remembered an old story that he could use to keep his kingdom...
“I will return your crown, Jason, if you go to Colchis and you bring back to me the Golden Fleece.”
Jason accepted the challenge. But when he left the King Pelias, he was overwhelmed with great worry; how indeed could a young man by himself and without assistance hope to go so far and overcome so many perils.
The old woman who has helped Jason was waiting for her at the exit of the palace. She was none other than Hera, the goddess of Zeus, the king of gods and had taken the appearance of a simple mortal to test him. Jason had some piety in helping her cross dangerous waters and had gained her affection. She would help him during his adventures.
Ce fils de Zeus, qui délivrera Prométhée, c’est Héraklès, le plus grand des héros grec, celui qui accomplira les fameux douze travaux.
The Argo
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
“build a great ship, she advises him. You will name it Argo. Then call to your help the greatest heroes of Greece. They will accompany you in your travels because they are seeking glory and adventure. When you will be together, no enemy, no monster nor any challenge shall vanquish you.”
Indeed many heroes responded to the call of Jason. Never in all of Greece had we seen such a group together for an adventure as crazy. They would become the navigators of the Argo, and they would be called the Argonauts.
Ce fils de Zeus, qui délivrera Prométhée, c’est Héraklès, le plus grand des héros grec, celui qui accomplira les fameux douze travaux.
The Argonauts
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Among them, the strongest was Herakles – also known under the name Hercules. This son of Zeus decided to take a break from his twelve Labours to accompany the Argo. He went on board with his companion: Hylas.
Tiresias then went on board. In his youth, he had seen Athena taking her bath, and Athena in anger had taken his sight. But in losing his eyes, Tiersias had become a seer and could foresee the future.
Next came the poet Orpheus, the greatest musician of all times. Then the two brothers Calais and Zetes who were sons of the god of wind and had wings like birds. And then boarded Theseus who had come back from Crete where he vanquished the Minotaur. Typhus, who would be the pilot joined the group and was followed by the twins Castor and Pollux, who like Hercules were sons of Zeus and the human Leda.
There were also many other heroes including Pelee, Telamon, OIlee and Laerte: their sons many years later participated in another expedition still greater. It was the Battle of Troy and that is another story.
